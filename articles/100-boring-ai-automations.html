Why boring wins

The profitable automations are rarely cinematic agent demos. They are the repeated workflows that leak attention, revenue, or trust: unanswered leads, missing follow-ups, messy handoffs, stale reports, unfiled decisions, manual copy-paste, and forgotten tasks.

A boring automation is worth building when it has:

  • a recurring trigger;
  • a clear owner;
  • a measurable cost of delay or error;
  • low risk if the first version only drafts or routes work;
  • an obvious human approval point.

How to choose the first automation

Use the ROI calculator and score each candidate:

  • frequency;
  • time per occurrence;
  • revenue impact;
  • error/rework rate;
  • failure risk;
  • data sensitivity;
  • approval requirement.

Start where automation drafts, summarises, routes or reminds. Delay anything that sends, spends, deletes, publishes, trades, or promises.
